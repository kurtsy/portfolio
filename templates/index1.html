ADVANCE LOCAL

As the UX Designer at Advance Local, I facilitate collaboration between the design and product team. I strive to balance the needs of writers, readers, stakeholders, and developers by advocating for a human-centered design approach.

Project: Photo Galleries and Slideshows

My work on Advance Local's photo galleries and multimedia slideshows is a case study in the art of balancing a variety of requirements. Oftentimes, user experience design is shown by a venn diagram with "UX" perfectly situated between the business and the user needs. But what happens when the business has more than one set of users and those users have competing needs? Rather than introducing another circle to the diagram, I've found that aligning a company's success metric around a unified user will result in a better user experience. And, sometimes, that "success metric" isn't a single number. Sometimes it's not a number at all.

When I started at Advance, I was put in charge of their recently redesigned photo gallery and multimedia slideshow experiences. The designer before me had done an amazing job identifying a subpar design and making it better. The company moved away from a clickbait gallery structure into a scrollable experience. But people weren't getting into the new galleries. Now that we were getting users into the galleries, we had to deal with the quality of the experience as readers consuemd content.
